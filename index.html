Pollution in Every Drop

A Community Service Project

Week 1: Initial Survey and Problem Identification

Activities: Conducted preliminary visits to water bodies in the community, collected initial water samples, and interacted with residents to understand their concerns.

Key Findings: Water sources showed visible signs of pollution, including turbidity, foul odors, and algal blooms. Residents reported frequent health issues such as diarrhea and skin infections.

Outcome: Established a foundation for detailed investigations in the subsequent weeks.

Conclusion

Over the course of eight weeks, this project highlighted the severity of water pollution and its far-reaching impacts. By engaging the community, identifying key contributors, and proposing actionable solutions, we have laid the groundwork for a cleaner and healthier future.
